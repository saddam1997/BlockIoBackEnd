What are Testnets?

Testnets exist for every coin, such as Bitcoin, Litecoin, and Dogecoin. They are, however, completely separate from the main networks, where currency holds real monetary value.

Testnets have their own separate addresses, and coins. A Bitcoin Testnet coin, for example, cannot be transferred to a Bitcoin address. Testnet coins have no value, and are not traded for money like Bitcoin, Litecoin, and Dogecoin are. Payments are never made in Testnet coins.

Testnets exist for the sole purpose of testing applications while using coins that do not hold monetary value. They are meant for developers to debug their implementations in a safe, sandbox environment, so that if developers make mistakes, these mistakes are not costly. Contrast this with making mistakes (such as transferring the wrong amount of coins to a destination address) on the main network, where every mistake costs you real money.

Block.io provides you 3 different Testnets, one for each main network (Bitcoin, Litecoin, and Dogecoin). We provide you with Testnet coins so you can test your implementations in a sandbox environment.

Security Warning

Forwarding Addresses are useful if the sender thinks your Block.io wallet address is invalid. Block.io uses Multi Signature addresses, and some wallets/sites do not yet recognize this new standard. In contrast, Forwarding Addresses are Single Signature addresses, and recognized globally by sites that deal in Bitcoin.

By using a Forwarding Address, you are asking Block.io to forward funds from it to your Block.io Wallet. The new forwarding address is not a part of your Wallet, and is only a proxy to your Wallet.

You will have the private key to access this address on your own if you wish. Block.io will also need this private key to forward funds to your Wallet. This means Block.io will have access to funds in this forwarding address alongside yourself, and you will not have solitary control of your own funds until they are transferred to your Wallet from the forwarding address.
